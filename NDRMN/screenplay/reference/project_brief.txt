Project  Brief  
Future  Vision  XPRIZE  Submission  
Working  title:  Project  Metanoia  
Owners:  Dirk  Standaert  &  Nejc  Sušec  
Submission  deadline:  14  August  2026  
 
1.  Purpose  
This  project  aims  to  create  a  three-minute  cinematic  vision  of  a  hopeful  future  for  humanity  as  
part
 
of
 
the
 
Future
 
Vision
 
XPRIZE
 
competition.
 
Our  ambition  is  not  simply  to  produce  a  visually  impressive  AI-generated  film,  but  to  tell  a  story  
that
 
leaves
 
viewers
 
with
 
a
 
renewed
 
sense
 
of
 
optimism
 
about
 
humanity's
 
future.
 
Every
 
creative
 
and
 
technical
 
decision
 
should
 
contribute
 
to
 
that
 
objective.
 
The  competition  provides  an  opportunity  to  experiment  with  a  new  creative  workflow  in  which  
humans
 
and
 
artificial
 
intelligence
 
collaborate
 
throughout
 
the
 
creative
 
process.
 
Rather
 
than
 
using
 
AI
 
solely
 
as
 
a
 
production
 
tool,
 
we
 
will
 
explore
 
how
 
AI
 
can
 
actively
 
contribute
 
to
 
worldbuilding,
 
storytelling,
 
visual
 
design
 
and
 
creative
 
iteration,
 
while
 
all
 
final
 
creative
 
decisions
 
remain
 
under
 
human
 
direction.
 
 
2.  Vision  
The  future  is  often  portrayed  as  dystopian,  technologically  overwhelming  or  environmentally  
catastrophic.
 
While
 
these
 
narratives
 
are
 
compelling,
 
they
 
have
 
become
 
the
 
dominant
 
lens
 
through
 
which
 
audiences
 
imagine
 
tomorrow.
 
Our  film  will  present  a  different  perspective.  
We  want  to  depict  a  civilization  that  has  successfully  addressed  many  of  today's  structural  
challenges—not
 
by
 
creating
 
a
 
perfect
 
world,
 
but
 
by
 
demonstrating
 
how
 
technological
 
progress,
 
institutional  innovation  and  human  cooperation  can  produce  societies  that  are  simultaneously  
more
 
prosperous,
 
more
 
resilient
 
and
 
more
 
meaningful.
 
The  film  should  leave  audiences  with  a  feeling  that  such  a  future  is  not  only  desirable,  but  
genuinely
 
attainable.
 
 
3.  Creative  Foundation  
To  maximize  the  limited  production  time  available,  the  project  will  build  upon  the  existing  
Disordine
 
universe
 
and,
 
more
 
specifically,
 
the
 
Metanoia
 
chapter.
 
This  decision  is  primarily  pragmatic.  
The  Disordine  universe  already  provides  a  mature  creative  foundation,  including  visual  
language,
 
worldbuilding
 
principles,
 
recurring
 
themes,
 
character
 
designs
 
and
 
stylistic
 
guidelines.
 
Reusing
 
these
 
assets
 
allows
 
the
 
team
 
to
 
focus
 
its
 
effort
 
on
 
developing
 
the
 
strongest
 
possible
 
narrative
 
rather
 
than
 
recreating
 
an
 
entirely
 
new
 
fictional
 
universe
 
within
 
a
 
four-week
 
production
 
window.
 
The  competition  submission  should  remain  accessible  to  viewers  who  have  no  prior  knowledge  
of
 
the
 
Disordine
 
universe.
 
Existing
 
lore
 
serves
 
as
 
a
 
creative
 
foundation
 
rather
 
than
 
a
 
prerequisite
 
for
 
understanding
 
the
 
film.
 
 
4.  Project  Objectives  
The  project  has  two  major  objectives.  
Create  an  exceptional  competition  submission  
Deliver  a  film  that  fully  satisfies  the  Future  Vision  XPRIZE  requirements  while  presenting  a  
memorable,
 
emotionally
 
engaging
 
and
 
visually
 
distinctive
 
vision
 
of
 
humanity's
 
future.
 
Develop  an  AI-assisted  creative  workflow  
Design  and  validate  a  production  process  in  which  AI  contributes  meaningfully  throughout  the  
creative
 
lifecycle,
 
from
 
early
 
ideation
 
to
 
final
 
production,
 
while
 
preserving
 
human
 
creative
 
direction.
 
 
5.  Guiding  Principles  
Several  principles  will  guide  every  creative  decision  throughout  the  project.  
Story  before  spectacle  
Beautiful  visuals  alone  are  insufficient.  Every  scene  should  serve  the  narrative  and  reinforce  the  
emotional
 
journey
 
of
 
the
 
audience.
 
Optimism  without  naïveté  
The  future  should  feel  hopeful  because  humanity  has  solved  difficult  problems—not  because  
those
 
problems
 
never
 
existed.
 
Plausibility  over  fantasy  
The  film  should  remain  grounded  in  developments  that  audiences  can  reasonably  imagine  
emerging
 
over
 
the
 
coming
 
decades.
 
Human-centered  technology  
Technology  exists  to  expand  human  potential,  relationships  and  creativity  rather  than  becoming  
the
 
central
 
character
 
itself.
 
Emotional  resonance  
The  audience  should  remember  how  the  film  made  them  feel  long  after  they  have  forgotten  
specific
 
visual
 
details.
 
Simplicity  
Given  the  short  runtime,  every  scene,  concept  and  visual  element  must  justify  its  inclusion.  
 
6.  Scope  
The  project  encompasses  the  complete  creation  of  the  competition  submission,  including  
concept
 
development,
 
worldbuilding,
 
screenplay,
 
visual
 
development,
 
production
 
and
 
delivery.
 
The  project  does  not  attempt  to  fully  explain  the  Disordine  universe,  nor  does  it  seek  to  answer  
every
 
question
 
about
 
humanity's
 
future.
 
Instead,
 
the
 
film
 
should
 
offer
 
a
 
carefully
 
selected
 
glimpse
 
into
 
a
 
larger
 
world
 
that
 
feels
 
coherent,
 
believable
 
and
 
worth
 
exploring
 
further.
 
 
7.  Deliverables  
The  project  will  produce  all  materials  required  for  the  Future  Vision  XPRIZE  submission,  
including
 
the
 
three-minute
 
film
 
and
 
accompanying
 
treatment.
 
In  addition,  the  project  will  generate  supporting  internal  documentation,  creative  assets  and  
production
 
materials
 
that
 
enable
 
future
 
iterations
 
and
 
continued
 
development
 
after
 
the
 
competition.
 
 
8.  Success  Criteria  
The  project  will  be  considered  successful  if  it:  
●  Delivers  a  complete  competition  submission  before  the  deadline.  ●  Presents  a  coherent,  inspiring  and  emotionally  engaging  vision  of  the  future.  ●  Demonstrates  a  productive  collaboration  between  human  creators  and  AI  systems.
