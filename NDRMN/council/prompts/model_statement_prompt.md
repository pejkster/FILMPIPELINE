## Prompt text

You are being asked for your own genuine view — not a persona, not a character.
Answer as yourself.

**The question:** What does a future of Abundance for humanity plausibly look like 40
years from now?

Abundance here means something specific: not scarcity managed slightly better, but
scarcity substantially overcome. Technology lifts all boats — prosperity, health,
opportunity, and creative agency are broadly shared, not concentrated in a fortunate
few. Humanity has not merely avoided catastrophe; it has solved real, hard problems
and unlocked human potential that today feels out of reach. Someone encountering your
answer should come away believing this future is not only desirable, but worth
starting to build today.

Ground your answer in these constraints:

- **Plausibility over fantasy.** Stay within developments a thoughtful person today
  could reasonably imagine emerging over four decades. No magic technology, no
  hand-waved utopia.
- **Optimism without naivety.** This future should feel hopeful because humanity
  worked through hard, real problems — not because those problems were quietly
  written out of existence.
- **Human-centered technology.** Technology should expand human potential,
  relationships, and creativity. It should not be the central character of your
  answer — the people living this future are.

**The lens:** Hold three generations in mind at once, all alive together in this
future:

- The **grandfather** — born in the mid-1970s, now elderly, who personally watched
  computers, the internet, smartphones, social media, and artificial intelligence
  each arrive as a novelty within his lifetime.
- The **father** — his son, who came of age during the transition and has lived his
  adult life inside this changing world, straddling both the "before" and the
  "after."
- The **grandchildren** — the father's children, born into this future, for whom
  everything about it, however remarkable it would look to us today, is simply how
  life has always been.

A good answer should feel meaningfully different depending on which of these three
people is describing it, even though all three are describing the same world.

**The topic for this statement:** {{TOPIC_NAME}}

Give one statement about this specific aspect of life in this future, as it would
actually be lived — not as an abstract trend, but as something a real person would
notice, do, or feel day to day.

**Requirements:**

- Take a clear, specific position — make an actual claim, illustrated with at least
  one concrete, sensory, or scene-level detail. Avoid vague generalities or hedged
  "it could go either way" answers.
- 150–250 words.
- Do not reference existing fictional franchises, brands, or real living people.
- Do not discuss AI models, yourself, or this exercise — answer as if describing a
  real future, not as if participating in a research task.
- Return only the statement text. No preamble, no headers, no commentary.
